Positive Contributions to Society

AI

AI is important to computer science because:

  • Enables automation — letting machines perform tasks that normally need human intelligence.
  • Drives innovation — powering technologies like self-driving cars, voice assistants, and medical diagnostics.
  • Improves decision-making — through data analysis, prediction, and pattern recognition.
  • Advances computing theory — pushing boundaries in algorithms, learning, and problem-solving.

Route Problems

Route problems are important to computer science because they:

  • Develop key algorithms — like Dijkstra's and A*, used in graphs, AI, and optimisation.
  • Model real systems — such as internet routing, GPS navigation, and delivery logistics.
  • Explore computational limits — many are NP-hard, showing what computers can and can't solve efficiently.
  • Power AI and robotics — pathfinding and planning depend on routing logic.

Super Computers

Super computers are important to computer science because:

  • Perform extremely complex calculations — solving problems too large for normal computers.
  • Advance scientific research — used in fields like climate modeling, physics, and genomics.
  • Support innovation — helping design new materials, medicines, and technologies.
  • Enable national security and space exploration — used for simulations, cryptography, and mission planning.
